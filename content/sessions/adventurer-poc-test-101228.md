The harbor smelled of salt and bureaucratic ink — a combination unique to the Shardsea of Governance, where even the tide seemed to move with a sense of procedure. The *Celestine* — or whatever name her crew had settled on this week — rocked gently in her berth as the four adventurers stepped ashore for the first time into the third great Shardsea, their boots finding cobblestones worn smooth by a thousand merchant negotiations and naval parades. After the primal fury of elemental islands and the spiritual rot of Nature's Wrath, this place felt almost deceptively civilized. Almost.

Rides the Wake took stock of the dockside with the unhurried patience of someone who had learned that new ports reveal themselves to those who wait. Granit stood beside her like a carved thing, reading the faces of harbor officials with the same quiet attention they gave to scripture. Lyvriele catalogued the architecture — the placement of customs houses, the lines of sight between guard towers — committing it all to memory before the city had a chance to rearrange itself. Vargr simply listened, the way he always listened, letting the ambient noise of the place settle into something that might eventually mean something.

They had arrived with full provisions for now, though the matter of rations, charts, and ship's maintenance still pressed. The Shardsea of Governance was not a place that rewarded unprepared travelers — it rewarded those who had done the paperwork. Somewhere in these waters, an objective waited, its shape not yet clear: something about authority bending wrong, or law weaponized, or the machinery of civilization grinding people beneath it. The pattern was consistent enough to be worrying. Each Shardsea had taught them something the last one hadn't prepared them for.

The leylines haunted the back of every thought — that vision from Glaciarra still fresh, the dark purple corruption threading through the world's veins like poison in a river. Whether Governance was cause or symptom remained to be seen. But the Compass of Lost Tides had not steered them wrong yet, and it had brought them here. That was enough, for now, to take the next step.
