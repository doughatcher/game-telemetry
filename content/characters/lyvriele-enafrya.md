Lyvriele Enafrya moves through the world with the particular bearing of someone who has learned that elegance and survival are not mutually exclusive.

An Eladrin fighter trained in the Samurai tradition — a discipline as much about presence as technique, about the unsheathed quality of attention a warrior brings to everything, not just combat. The Samurai does not simply react to a fight. They have already considered it. Lyvriele does not draw her blade slowly because she is hesitant. She draws it slowly because the time spent not drawing it is when she has already decided.

Shards A and B asked her to be precise in imprecise conditions — overgrown, feral, corrupted spaces where footing was uncertain and the threats did not respect spacing or timing. She adapted. Eladrin Fey Step is an ability that sounds elegant in a tavern and proves essential in a forest that is trying to kill you, or a Shard that has stopped behaving like itself. Lyvriele arrived at Shard C fully rested and better equipped than she left the last one, her AC a measure harder to breach than before.

The Sea of Governance suggests a theater where appearances and authority carry real force. If anything, this is closer to Lyvriele's native register — she understands the weight of bearing, of what it communicates before a word is spoken. Whether what governs here can be reasoned with, she has not yet had occasion to test. She is, as always, already thinking about it.
