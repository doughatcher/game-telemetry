Granit is a name that suits someone who does not move unless they mean to. A dwarf cleric of the Life Domain — healer, anchor, the kind of steadiness a party leans on before they know they're leaning. There is an economy to how Granit operates: no wasted gestures, no unnecessary words. The sacred power moving through them is not flashy, but it is deep, and it shows up when it is needed.

They arrived at Shard C, Sea of Governance, alongside the others — and that silence of theirs said as much as any speech could. The others spoke of gods and duties and the growing weight of what threatens civilization itself. Granit offered something quieter: the sense that someone who has made peace with their purpose does not need to announce it. Whatever the gods have sanctioned, whatever the mission requires, Granit will be there when the accounting comes.

The long rest is done. Attunements have been made. The party is as prepared as it is going to be. That is the kind of moment Granit was built for — not the arrival, not the glory, but the quiet work of making sure everyone is still standing when it matters.
