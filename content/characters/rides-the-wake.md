Rides the Wake carries herself like someone accustomed to moving through rough water — patient in stillness, overwhelming in motion.

She is not reckless in the way people imagine barbarians to be. There is a current of deliberate purpose beneath the fury, a sense that the rage, when it comes, has been earned. Those who travel with her describe a quiet confidence that borders on the serene, right up until it doesn't. The name fits: she goes where the force has been and holds the line in the turbulence that follows.

In Shard A the answer to most problems was direct and physical, and Rides the Wake provided that without complaint. In Shard B, where corruption ran deeper and the threats were less honest about what they were, she adapted — the Berserker's tools are blunt but they are not stupid, and neither is she. She arrived at Shard C whole, rested, and carrying none of the Exhaustion that Frenzy sometimes leaves behind. That is either discipline or patience, and with her the distinction is hard to draw.

The Sea of Governance is a place built for people who argue with words. Rides the Wake is not that. But every system of order — no matter how elaborate its justifications — has a place where the argument stops and the weight of things takes over. She will be ready for that moment, as she has always been.
