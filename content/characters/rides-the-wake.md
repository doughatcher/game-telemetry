Rides the Wake carries herself like someone accustomed to moving through rough water — patient in stillness, overwhelming in motion.

She is not reckless in the way people imagine barbarians to be. There is a current of deliberate purpose beneath the fury, a sense that the rage, when it comes, has been earned. Those who travel with her describe a quiet confidence that borders on the serene, right up until it doesn't. The name fits: she goes where the force has been and holds the line in the turbulence that follows.

In Shard A — the Wild Reaches — the answer to most problems was direct and physical. The ancient forest and its feral guardians required something capable of absorbing enormous punishment while delivering worse in return. Rides the Wake provided that. Path of the Berserker means Frenzy is available — bonus action attacks, sustained offensive pressure — at the cost of Exhaustion afterward. She does not use it casually. In Shard B, where corruption ran deeper and the threats resisted simple solutions, that same discipline held. She arrived at Shard C unblooded and unhurried, rested and ready in the way only someone genuinely comfortable with what is coming can be.

The Sea of Governance is a different kind of arena. Whatever rules this Shard, it is not raw nature or festering corruption — it is structure, authority, the weight of civilizations trying to hold themselves together against something that does not negotiate. Rides the Wake has not said what she thinks of that yet. She rarely announces her thinking. She waits, reads the current, and moves when it is time.
