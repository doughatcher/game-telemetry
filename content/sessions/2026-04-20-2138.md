The waters changed before the ship did. Something in the air grew heavier as the vessel pressed on — a pressure behind the eyes, a stillness that had no business sitting atop open sea. Then the horizon offered up its answer: the Shard C Sea of Governance, vast and ancient, its surface catching light in ways that seemed almost deliberate, as though the ocean itself were aware of being watched in return.

Rides the Wake stood at the prow as they crossed into those strange waters, the human barbarian's broad frame unmoving against the salt wind. Behind him, Granit muttered something low in the old Dwarven tongue — a prayer, perhaps, or a warning dressed as one. The Life Domain cleric had seen enough of the world to know that power this old rarely asked permission before making itself felt. The energies here were not hostile, not yet, but they were not indifferent either. They pressed against the hull and curled through the rigging like fingers testing the measure of what had arrived.

Lyvriele Enafrya kept her hand near her blade out of habit more than fear, her Eladrin eyes tracking the currents below the surface where shapes of light moved with unsettling purpose. Beside her, Vargr Stormcaller had gone uncharacteristically quiet, the Goliath bard listening — truly listening — as though the sea were whispering something just below the pitch a human ear could catch. Whatever it was, he did not yet share it.

They made anchor before the full dark came down, and the crew took what rest they could. Tomorrow would demand sharp weapons, full bellies, and sharper minds. The Sea of Governance had received them without violence, but no one aboard was foolish enough to mistake silence for welcome. Whatever law or power had named these waters still waited somewhere beneath, patient as tide, certain as depth.
