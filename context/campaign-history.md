## Setting

A world of fractured seas and ancient power, where civilization's grip is held not by armies alone but by the contested authority of **Shardseas** — vast geographic and metaphysical territories, each saturated with a different kind of fundamental force. The world is navigated by ship, politics, and survival. The party operates as adventurers with enough reputation to be trusted with matters of civilization-level consequence.

### The Shardsea System

The world is organized around Shardseas — regions of concentrated elemental or conceptual power. A Shardsea is an archipelago, containing a number of different, small islands. The party has traversed:

- **Shardsea of Elements** *(visited, cleared)*
  Nature in its most primal and dangerous form. Ancient forests, feral spirits, and ecological power older than cities. The party's first major trial. Whatever they faced here forged their working dynamic — Granit's steady anchor, Rides the Wake's controlled fury, Lyvriele's methodical precision, Vargr's connective tissue between action and meaning.

  The party first sailed to Pyroskarn, an island with an erupting volcano where the players had to slay an ancient elemental to calm down the raging inferno. They next sailed to Zephyrine, where they restored the power of an ancient weather control device and slew a wind serpent, drawn by the elemental chaos, that was causing horrible storms. Finally they party sailed to Glaciarra, where they encountered an ancient yeti that was once a defender of the island's people but had somehow become corrupted.

  After slaying Frostmaul the Devourer, the Compass of Lost Tides flared to light and shows the party a vision. They say the arcane leylines scattered throughout the world, but many of them were corrupted with some kind of dark purple miasma spreading across the world. 

- **Shardsea of Nature's Wrath** *(visited, cleared)*
  Something had gone wrong here at a fundamental level — corruption not of bodies but of essence. The second shard tested the party differently: not just combat capability but moral and spiritual resilience. Vargr's College of Spirits connection was particularly relevant here; the dead speak differently in corrupted ground.

  They players first sailed to the island of Feyblossom, where the energies of the Feywild started to bleed through into the Material Plane. After defeating a possessed puppet and reclaiming their names, they sailed to the island of Verdantreach that had been overtaken by nature and completely abandoned by the native population. They eventually found a number of creatures, including a large owlbear, that had almost fused with the corrupted nature itself. Finally the party sailed to Tidecross, where rising tides kept the island almost perpetually flooded. The players sailed to the four shrines to repair them, summoning the ocean spirit Vaeloryn, and eventually slaying it. 

- **Sharsea of Governance** *(current location)*
  Power in its most human and institutional form. A vast maritime political zone where trade routes, naval authority, and shard-based power structures intersect. Shard C is not dangerous the way raw nature or corruption is dangerous — it is dangerous the way bureaucracy and compromise and competing interests are dangerous. Named for governance because whoever controls these waters controls civilization's nervous system.

## Campaign Tone & Rules

- **Gritty but heroic.** Combat is dangerous at level 7. Resources matter.

- **The world moves.** Delays have costs. Information has a shelf life.
- **NPCs have agendas.** Not everyone is an enemy, not everyone is an ally, most are somewhere in between.

## World Details

- **Travel is by sea.** The party arrived at Shardsea by ship. Maritime logistics (rations, charts, ship condition) are real concerns.
- **The Shardsea system implies a cosmology.** Each shardsea is a concentration of fundamental force. Someone or something created or maintains this system — or it's natural and the civilizations of the world have simply organized around it.
- **Governance = power.** The Sea of Governance is named for a reason. Control of these waters means control of trade, communication, and military projection across the known world.
- **Vargr can talk to the dead.** College of Spirits means the history of places is accessible through séance and channeling. This has been relevant before and will be again.
- **Edwin was a fifth member who departed.** The party is now four. His absence is presumably known within the world.
