Vargr Stormcaller gives the impression of someone who has seen enough of the world to be skeptical of it, but not so much as to have stopped caring. A Goliath bard of the College of Spirits — a strange combination that somehow makes complete sense once you encounter it. The spirits speak to those who are large enough to hold them, and Vargr has always been that.

At the threshold of Shard C, with the Sea of Governance spreading out before the party, Vargr was the one drawing connections — tracing the shape of what this place might mean in the larger pattern of things. The first shard touched nature, raw and violent. The second corrupted something deeper. This one, it seems, reaches into civilization itself, into the structures by which people agree to be governed at all. Vargr named this aloud in the way bards do: not because no one else had thought it, but because someone had to say it.

The name "Stormcaller" suggests a history with weather — perhaps literal, perhaps metaphorical. Either way, it is a name for someone who has learned to work with forces larger than themselves rather than against them. The Sea of Governance is exactly that kind of force. Vargr will not be surprised by it. That may be the most dangerous thing about him.
